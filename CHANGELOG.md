# Changelog

All notable changes to Moongate, newest first. Versions follow `vMAJOR.MINOR.PATCH+BUILD` where `BUILD` is a monotonic integer used by the in-app update banner.

| Version | Changes |
|---|---|
| **v0.9.14** | **Restore now reproduces your exact dashboard.** Restoring a backup used to only *add* printers that weren't already present — it ignored the saved order and per-tile settings of printers you already had. Now restore is authoritative: it rebuilds the dashboard to match the backup exactly — the saved **printer order** and each tile's config (including **hidden-webcam / compact** tiles), adding any missing printers and (after confirming) removing any that aren't in the backup. Because removing is destructive, if your phone has printers the backup doesn't include, restore **asks first and lists them** before replacing — so you can't silently lose a printer you paired after the backup was made. On a fresh install there's nothing to remove, so it just restores as before. Removed printers stay paired (it's a local change), so they can be re-added or restored later. App-only update |
| **v0.9.13** | **Drag-to-reorder the printer tiles is back.** v0.9.12 (the per-printer Webcams / masonry update) temporarily swapped the dashboard's **drag-the-tiles** reordering for a plain single-column list. This restores the original workflow: turn off **"Auto-arrange by status"** in the menu, tap the **drag-drop button** (bottom-left, opposite the +), then **drag the tiles around the dashboard** and tap the green **check** to lock them in. Both **full** and **compact** (webcam-hidden) tiles can be dragged; while arranging, the tiles sit in a uniform grid (alignment isn't the point there) and the tidy **masonry packing returns** the moment you finish. Your custom order is saved and travels in your backups, as before. App-only update |
| **v0.9.12** | **Show or hide each printer's webcam + see-through tiles.** **(1) Webcams.** The menu's **Webcams** entry now opens a sheet listing every printer, each with an on/off switch. Turn one **off** and its dashboard tile collapses to a **compact card** — name, connection, status and temperatures, with **no camera** — and the dashboard **packs the full and compact tiles together** (a masonry grid), so the printers you're not actively watching take up far less room. A hidden webcam also **stops that tile fetching its camera**, so it's a per-printer **data saver** too — this replaces the previous all-or-nothing "Show webcams" toggle with per-printer control. **(2) Tile opacity.** On the **Custom theme**, a new **Tile opacity** (0–100) lets your dashboard background show **through** the tiles, while the camera feed stays solid. **(3) Translations.** The **Advanced Power Switch** and the new tile-opacity controls are now translated across all **8 languages**. App-only update |
| **v0.9.11** | **Clear a finished print from the tile + a custom dashboard background.** **(1) Dismiss a finished job.** When a print ends, its tile shows a **Done** (or **Cancelled**) badge — tap the small **×** on it to clear the job and send the printer back to **Idle**. One tap, no confirm (the × only appears on those finished states, so a stray tap can't disturb a live print), and the print notification follows to Idle on its next poll. Under the hood it sends Klipper's `SDCARD_RESET_FILE` through the existing proxy. **(2) Custom dashboard background.** On the **Custom theme**, the theme screen gains a **Dashboard background** option — pick any image from your phone to sit behind the tiles. It's **centred and scaled to fit over your theme colour**, so a right-sized photo fills the screen, a wrong-aspect one is centred (letterboxed), and a logo PNG sits in the middle with the theme colour showing around and through it. The **×** clears it. **(3) Refreshed icon guide.** The in-app guide (menu → About) now documents all the newer tile buttons — Print a file, Macros, Power, Lighting, Camera, Camera setup — and the new clear-a-print ×. **(4) Appearance & menu tidy-up.** The cold-start loading screen now matches the **dark** theme — dark, with the red **Moongate** brand mark, and no white flash on a light-mode phone; the **System default** theme option is removed (themes are now **Dark / Light / Custom**, default Dark); and **Settings + Language** scroll with the rest of the menu, leaving only the **version** pinned at the bottom. **(5) Advanced Power Switch.** A new menu screen (beside Lighting) for printers whose power is a **Klipper macro** rather than a Moonraker `[power]` device — set an **On + Off** pair or a single **Toggle** macro, and the tile's power button drives the printer through it. With on/off macros it asks **Turn on / Turn off** (the state can't be read, so it never assumes); the light bulb behaves the same when it has on/off macros and no toggle or status source. App-only update |
| **v0.9.10** | **Power your printer from its tile + tidier idle tiles.** **(1) Power on/off button.** If a printer has a Moonraker **power device** (a `[power …]` section — any type: GPIO, Shelly, TP-Link, Tasmota…), a **power button** now appears in the **bottom-left** of its camera tile. Tap it and **confirm** to switch the printer on or off. It works **even when the printer is off** (Moonraker stays up), so you can wake a powered-down printer right from its tile; powering off is blocked while a print is running for devices Moonraker locks. Detected automatically — no setup. **(2) Tidier idle tiles.** A printer that's offline or idle (Klipper not running) now shows **just its name** instead of a meaningless `0°/0°` temperature row. **(3) Show-webcams switch.** A new **Show webcams** toggle (menu → Camera feed) turns **all** dashboard camera feeds off or on at once — a quick data saver that also lets on-demand cameras (go2rtc) idle; the full-screen camera view still works on demand. App-only update |
| **v0.9.9** | **Finish-time ETA in notifications + a notification you can customise.** **(1) When will it finish?** The live print notification now shows the **projected finish time** as a clock time (e.g. **1:20 PM**), right next to the time remaining — the same **ETA** Klipper and Mainsail display. It follows your app's language for 12- or 24-hour format. **(2) Make the notification yours.** A new **Notification content** screen (under **Print notifications** in the menu) lets you choose **which details appear** — progress, time remaining, finish time, hotend temperature, bed temperature — **drag them into any order**, and switch off the ones you don't want (e.g. show the finish time but hide the running time). A **live preview** updates as you tweak, and your choice is **saved and travels in your backups**. **(3) Tidier menu.** The **Lighting** entry now sits right under **Font size** for quicker reach. App-only update |
| **v0.9.8** | **Instant printer re-open + lighting control.** **(1) Re-opening a printer is now instant.** A printer's full Mainsail/Fluidd interface used to reload from scratch every time you opened its tile — replaying Mainsail's **"Initializing…"** while the page and its live connection rebuilt, which is slow over the tunnel (away from home). Now each printer's page is **kept warm in the background**: the first open loads it as before, but afterwards, going back to the dashboard and re-opening is **instant**, with the connection still live. Pages are kept for every printer you've opened; if the phone runs low on memory, the least-recently-used ones are released automatically. **(2) Control your printer lights from the dashboard.** Set up which printers have a controllable light under the new **Lighting** menu — for each, choose an **On + Off** macro pair *or* a single **Toggle** macro (whichever your setup uses), and optionally point Moongate at the light's Klipper object (e.g. `output_pin caselight`). A **bulb** then appears in the corner of that tile's camera: tap it to switch the light on/off, and it glows when the light is on — reflecting the real state even if you toggle it elsewhere. App-only update |
| **v0.9.7** | **Squarer, tidier dashboard tiles.** Each printer tile's live **camera feed is now a clean 1:1 square** — it was cropped to an odd wide-and-short shape before — with the name and temperatures tucked underneath. **Offline printers** no longer leave a blank gap: an unreachable tile **fills with its Mainsail/Fluidd logo** and shows just the **name** (there are no live temperatures to report while offline), so it sits flush instead of stretching. And while a printer is **printing**, the **macro button hides** so the progress bar and percentage keep their width and stay easy to read — it returns once the tile is idle or finished. App-only update |

Internal **helper macros** (names starting with `_`) and Moongate's own **pairing macros** (`MOONGATE_PAIR` / `MOONGATE_RESET_OWNER`) are hidden to keep the list to the macros you'd actually run. Also tidied the tile controls — the firmware-restart button now shows only when a printer has errored, not on every idle tile. App-only update |

| **v0.5.0** | **LAN discovery via mDNS — survives IP changes and internet outages.** Companion to the v0.4.4 Pi-side mDNS advertisement. The app now browses for `_moongate._tcp.local` services on the home WiFi and uses each discovered URL as the authoritative LAN address for the matching printer. Effect: when your Pi's IP changes (DHCP renewal, router reboot, ISP-supplied gateway swap), the dashboard tile flips back to "Local" on the next poll cycle instead of waiting for a tunnel-mediated recovery — even if the internet leg is down. | **v0.4.4** | **Pi-side groundwork for LAN discovery (v0.5.0 preparation).** After a successful pair, the Pi now advertises itself on the local network via mDNS (Avahi) as `_moongate._tcp.local` with its `printer_id` and HTTP port. No user-visible behaviour change in this release on its own — the v0.5.0 app will browse for these advertisements and use them as the authoritative LAN address, so the dashboard tile stays "Local" through DHCP IP changes / router reboots even while the internet leg is down. To pick up the change, re-run the Pi installer (`curl -fsSL …/install.sh \| bash`) — it adds the required tightly-scoped sudoers entry and ensures `avahi-daemon` is enabled. App side unchanged; same v0.4.3 behaviour against a v0.4.3 Pi. See `docs/v0.5-lan-discovery-design.md` for the full design |
| **v0.4.3** | **Webcam tile fix for slow camera servers.** Dashboard webcam thumbnails were stuck on the Mainsail / Fluidd logo placeholder for any printer whose webcam server took longer than ~33 ms to return a snapshot — most commonly **stock RatRig Micron+ machines running uv4l-mjpeg**, where each snapshot can take 3 s. The tile's old fetch loop ticked at the configured 15–30 FPS and cancelled every in-flight `Image.network` load with the next tick, so no frame ever completed. The fetch loop is now strictly sequential: the next snapshot only kicks off after the previous one finishes (success, error, or timeout). Fast Crowsnest setups still hit the configured rate; slow ones self-throttle to whatever they can sustain instead of looking dead. Tunnel-mode 3 FPS hard cap removed in the same change — the new loop is naturally bounded by round-trip latency. Pi-side install is unchanged; just update the app |

| **v0.4.0** | **Hardened remote access.** Every internet-facing path on the Pi now sits behind a small auth proxy that requires a short-lived signed token on every request. Sharing the tunnel URL gives an attacker nothing — the URL returns flat 401s with no fingerprint hinting at what's behind it. This closes the "visit the tunnel URL in a browser and you see Mainsail" hole noted in v0.2.29's caveat. The full Mainsail / Fluidd UI is reachable through the tunnel from anywhere, including cellular. Dashboard tile gains a "Connected — Printer idle" state for when the Pi is up but the printer side isn't (e.g. K3 with its printer-power toggle off), and the detected web UI (Mainsail or Fluidd) is now persisted per printer so the logo shows immediately on a cold launch even before the first poll succeeds. Side-menu additions: a brief "What's new" dialog (this page in app form) and a "Buy me a coffee" link |
| **v0.3.0** | **Cloud-mediated pairing.** Replaces the v0.2 shared-URL-plus-local-token model with a small cloud middleman that handles anonymous sign-in (no email, no password — the app creates an anonymous identity on first launch), tracks the current tunnel URL per Pi (Cloudflare Quick Tunnel URLs rotate every Pi reboot), and issues a fresh short-lived access token to the app on demand. End-user impact: nothing to share, no URL to remember, no tunnel-URL re-pairing when the Pi reboots. The Remove printer flow now releases the cloud association so re-pairing the same Pi just works. Hard cutover from v0.2.x — fresh install required; v0.2.x local state moved aside to `~/.config/moongate/legacy-v0.2.x/` |
| **v0.2.29** | Incident-response improvements after the "shared the tunnel URL with a friend and they got control" scenario: (1) QR pre-issued tokens are now **provisional** — they carry a 10-minute initial TTL even though the JWT itself is signed for 30 days, and only "promote" to the full 30 days on first actual use. A shared but unscanned URL self-expires inside 10 minutes instead of 30 days. (2) New `MOONGATE_REVOKE_ALL` macro for one-shot token nuking after an incident. (3) New `MOONGATE_LIST_TOKENS` macro that prints every issued token (active/expired/revoked) to the Mainsail console for auditing. (4) `MOONGATE_PAIR` console output now carries a prominent "⚠ DO NOT SHARE THIS URL" warning block.
